CASE STUDY 
INSURANCE PRODUCT COMPARISON 
 
Overview 
 
This case study aims to evaluate your ability to build scalable tools, tailored for 
the AIA, to solve an actual business problem. Read the below problem statement, instructions, 
acceptance criteria, and deliverables carefully.  
 
Your solution will be primarily evaluated on the tool’s ability to address the outlined business pain 
points. Secondary evaluation criteria will focus on the technical implementation, including scalability, 
robustness, but also creativity and overall technical approach. 
 
Overall the tool should be presented as a fully functional working demo, either hosted locally or 
deployed on the cloud. You are also expected to document your approach and describe your solution 
approach, tech stack, and outline of your core features. 
 
Business Problem 
 
While AIA’s website currently offers a basic simple side-by-side comparison table for customers to 
evaluate and differentiate between multiple critical illness products, customers often face difficulties 
when trying to compare more detailed aspects. Specifically, each plan includes a wide range of 
features, such as premium payment structures, inpatient and outpatient coverage limits, benefits, 
deductibles, optional riders, and policy exclusions. As a result, customers frequently struggle to form a 
clear, side-by-side understanding of how two or more plans truly compare across these dimensions.  
 
This slows down the decision-making process for customers but also increases the likelihood of 
misunderstanding product benefits, leading to frustration, lower conversion rates, and additional 
support burden on AIA’s Sales Agents. 
 
To address this, the business is seeking to develop a web application that enables users to quickly 
and intuitively compare multiple AIA insurance products in one place. The solution should present key 
features in a standardized, easy-to-read format, allow dynamic selection of plans, highlight differences 
between product attributes, and ultimately empower customers to make more informed decisions with 
minimal friction. This comparison tool is expected to enhance customer experience, improve sales, 
and support more transparent product communication across the AIA portfolio.

Acceptance Criteria 
 
User Interface 
1.1.  Comparison Dashboard 
1.1.1.  The web application must provide a clean, intuitive dashboard that allows users to select 
at least two AIA insurance plans from a searchable drop-down menu. 
1.1.2.  Upon selecting plans, the system must automatically generate a side-by-side 
comparison table showing key product dimensions, including but not limited to:  
1.1.2.1.  Coverage categories (inpatient, outpatient, surgical, etc.) 
1.1.2.2.  Premium and payment mode notes 
1.1.2.3.  Annual limits and sub-limits 
1.1.2.4.  Deductible and co-pay structures 
1.1.2.5.  Riders and add-on benefits 
1.1.2.6.  Exclusions or special conditions 
1.1.3.  Each table field must include a citation reference, pointing to the exact brochure line, 
clause, or section number from which the data was extracted (e.g., “Source: Brochure 
v2025 – Section 3.2”). 
1.1.4.  Differences between plans should be visually highlighted (e.g., bold text, colored markers) 
to help users immediately identify how products diverge across categories. 
 
Chat Interaction Panel 
1.2.  A dedicated chat panel must allow users to ask follow-up, natural-language questions 
related to product differences.
